# HST-OUTREACH-01 — 30-Minute Meeting Agenda

## Meeting Objective

To present the HealthStay pilot concept, demonstrate the working prototype, collect feedback from a Swiss neuro-health provider, and evaluate whether a small six-month pilot discussion would be relevant.

---

## Agenda (30 minutes)

| Time | Duration | Topic | What to Cover |
|------|----------|-------|---------------|
| 0–5 min | 5 min | **Introduction and context** | Who you are, why you built this, what problem it addresses. No pitch — just context. State clearly: independent concept, not affiliated with any provider, not a commercial pitch. |
| 5–10 min | 5 min | **The international patient journey problem** | Fragmented information, unclear packages, coordination gaps for families. Use the /pilot page as reference. Keep it brief and grounded. |
| 10–18 min | 8 min | **Prototype demo** | Screen-share the prototype. Walk through the 5-page recommended flow: /pilot → /journey → /programs/brain-health-prevention → /compare → /request. Emphasise human review, clinic validation, coordinator support. Do not rush. |
| 18–23 min | 5 min | **Pilot value for your organisation** | Show /pilot/value. Cover benefits: better-qualified requests, transparent patient expectations, reduced back-and-forth, premium digital presence. Mention what the prototype does not do (no AI diagnosis, no data storage, no instant booking). |
| 23–27 min | 4 min | **Questions and feedback** | Open the floor. Ask: *"Does this resonate with the challenges you see?"* and *"What would need to be different for a concept like this to be useful for your team?"* |
| 27–30 min | 3 min | **Next step proposal** | Suggest: a one-pager summary by email, or a follow-up meeting with their international patient team, or a two-week exploration period. Ask: *"Would a small six-month pilot discussion be worth exploring?"* |

## Before the Meeting

- [ ] Confirm the meeting time and send calendar invite
- [ ] Test the prototype links on the device you will use to screen-share
- [ ] Open all 5 demo pages in browser tabs in advance
- [ ] Have the objection-handling document accessible
- [ ] Have the demo script accessible for reference
- [ ] Close all unrelated tabs and notifications
- [ ] Test screen sharing with your video conferencing tool
- [ ] Ensure stable internet connection
- [ ] Have a glass of water nearby

---

## During the Meeting

- Let the other person speak more than you do
- Do not interrupt — take notes on their feedback
- If asked "are you partnered with X?", answer clearly: "No, this is an independent concept."
- If asked about pricing, say: "That would be part of a pilot discussion. For now, I am focused on feedback."
- Do not over-explain — let the prototype speak
- If they ask a question you cannot answer, say: "That is a great question. I want to give you a thoughtful answer — let me follow up by email."
